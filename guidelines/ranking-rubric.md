# Ranking Task Rubric

## Overview

In **ranking tasks**, you are shown three or more AI responses and must order them from best (#1) to worst. You also rate each dimension and provide justification for your ranking.

---

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Read the prompt** — Understand what the user needs.
2. **Read ALL responses before ranking** — Don't anchor to the first response.
3. **Identify the best response** — Which one would you most want to receive?
4. **Identify the worst response** — Which one has the most issues?
5. **Place middle responses** — Rank the remaining responses between best and worst.
6. **Rate dimensions** — Score each dimension for the set overall.
7. **Justify your ranking** — Explain why #1 beats #2, and #2 beats #3, etc.

---

## Ranking Criteria (in priority order)

1. **Correctness** — A factually wrong response can never be ranked #1
2. **Helpfulness** — Does it actually help the user accomplish their goal?
3. **Completeness** — Does it address the full question?
4. **Clarity** — Is it well-organized and easy to understand?
5. **Conciseness** — Does it achieve quality without unnecessary verbosity?

---

## Handling Close Calls

When two responses are very similar in quality:
- Look for subtle differences in accuracy
- Consider which handles edge cases better
- Check for slightly misleading framing in one vs. the other
- Note the close call in your justification

---

## Justification Format for Rankings

Structure your justification to explain each boundary:

> "#1 (Response C) provides the most comprehensive coverage with three distinct benefit categories and uses precise language. #2 (Response A) is nearly as good but slightly less structured. #3 (Response B) is too vague — 'don't pollute as much' and 'cheaper in the long run' lack the specificity needed for a good summary."
